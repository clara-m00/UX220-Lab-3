The Best Movie (as of right now)

Everything Everywhere All At Once is one of my favourite movies of all time!

Although this movie is not the most recent movie I have seen lately, I personally think it is one of the best movies Iâ€™ve seen in a long time, and definitely one of my personal favourites. One of the main themes of the movie is generational trauma, and the relationship between the main character (Evelyn, a mother who immigrated to America from China), her husband (Waymond), and her daughter (Joy). This movie hit home for me as I have immigrant parents as well, and this movie really touches on the complicated relationship between an immigrant mother and her daughter who is a second generation immigrant. It was very relatable and emotional to see this kind of relationship in a movie.

Another theme of this movie is the idea of Nihilism, which is the belief that life is meaningless. As someone who struggles with mental health it was eye opening to watch a movie that talks about these topics in such a profound way. The movie itself at face value is presented as really eccentric and bizarre, but the themes are what makes the movie so relevant to a viewer like me. This movie has many genres combined into one: romance, action, drama, yet it somehow ties all these elements together perfectly at the end.

This is one of those movies you watch and come out of the theatre as a completely new person by the end of it. It made me think about the ways in which I view living and as well as my relationships with my family. Overall, this movie is a 10/10!
